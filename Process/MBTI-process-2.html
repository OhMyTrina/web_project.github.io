mbti

By Trina Fang

MBTI is a personality test that categorizes people into 16 types based on how they gain energy, receive information, make decisions, and organize their lives. This test uses four sets of letters to distinguish people: Extraversion (E) vs. Introversion (I), Sensing (S) vs. Intuition (N), Thinking (T) vs. Feeling (F), Judging (J) vs. Perceiving (P). Its purpose is not to measure people's skills or mental health; it is to provide a language to help us understand why different people tend to focus on certain things, communicate in certain ways, or feel comfortable in different environments.

Extraversion

Extraversion (E)

Draws energy from interaction, action, and the external world.

Introvesion

Introvesion (I)

Draws energy from reflection, depth, and time alone or in small groups.

Sensing

Sensing (S)

Focuses on concrete facts, details, and present realities, trusts the five senses.

Intuition

Intuition (N)

Looks for patterns, meanings, and future possibilities rather than just current facts.

Thinking

Thinking (T)

Prefers logic, consistency, and objective criteria when deciding.

Feeling

Feeling (F)

Prefers personal values, empathy, and impact on people when deciding.

Judging

Judging (J)

Likes structure, plans, and closure; prefers decided, organized environments.

Perceiving

Perceiving (P)

Prefers flexibility, spontaneity, and keeping options open.
